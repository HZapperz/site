The Founder

A Seiko shouldn't cost more than the watch you wish it was.

I started building watches because I couldn't find a Daytona that didn't cost more than a car. The dial inside isn't Swiss — it's a Seiko NH35, the most reliable automatic movement on the planet, the same one a Speedmaster homage starts with. The case, the bezel, the dial, the hands — all chosen by you, built by me.

Seven hundred watches in, I've learned that what people actually want isn't the cheapest mod, or the fanciest one. They want to know where their watch is. They want to talk to a real person. They want to open the box and feel like the wait was worth it. So that's what I'm building.

03

Five stages from your spec to your wrist. You see all of them.

Stage 01
Sourced
Your parts laid out on the bench, photographed with your build number. Confirmation that the right pieces are in front of me before I touch a screwdriver.
Stage 02
Assembled
Movement seated, dial fitted, hands installed under microscope. A short video of your build coming together — phone-shot, not staged.
Stage 03
Regulated
Movement on the timing machine. Adjusted to within ±15 seconds/day across five positions. The actual readout, photographed.
Stage 04
QC'd
Pressure tested. Crown, bezel, bracelet checked. Hero photography of the finished watch with your build card. The shot you'll want to send to your dad.
Stage 05
Shipped
Tracking + a short setup video. Includes a handwritten note, build card with serial number, and the strap tools you'll need to swap bands later.
Every stage hits your email and your dashboard. So you never have to ask “where's my watch?”
