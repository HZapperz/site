The Founder

A Seiko shouldn't cost more than the watch you wish it was.

I started building watches because I couldn't find a Daytona that didn't cost more than a car. The dial inside is a Seiko NH35 — the most reliable automatic movement on the planet. The case, the bezel, the hands — chosen by you, built by me, in my Texas workshop.

Seven hundred watches in, I've learned that what people actually want isn't the cheapest mod, or the fanciest. They want to know where their watch is. They want to talk to a real person. They want to open the box and feel like the wait was worth it. That's what we build.

03

Five stages from your spec to your wrist. You see all of them.

Stage I
Sourced
Your parts laid out on the bench, photographed with your build number. Confirmation that the right pieces are in front of me.
Stage II
Assembled
Movement seated, dial fitted, hands installed under microscope. A short video of the build coming together.
Stage III
Regulated
Movement on the timing machine. Adjusted to within ±15 sec/day across five positions. The actual readout, photographed.
Stage IV
QC'd
Pressure tested. Crown, bezel, bracelet checked. Hero photography of the finished watch with your build card.
Stage V
Shipped
Tracking, setup video, handwritten note. Build card with serial number. Strap tools for future swaps.
Every stage hits your email and your dashboard.
So you never have to ask "where's my watch?"
