The single biggest leak

The booking handoff. Interested buyers leave your polished, on-brand site and land in an off-brand "submit three preferred times and wait for us to call" flow. They never walk away with a confirmed appointment. Plumbing demand is time-sensitive and often urgent — every minute of callback lag is a job won by whichever competitor confirms first. That is the exact conversion gap I have closed before.

02What it's costing you

Four specific places revenue is leaving the building.

Gap 01 · No instant booking

The callback gate

Dispatch.me collects three preferred slots and promises a human will follow up. The buyer leaves uncertain. For urgent plumbing — a leak, a dead water heater — uncertainty sends them straight to the next result that books on the spot.

Gap 02 · No visible proof

4.6 stars, hidden

You have 103 real Google reviews at 4.6 stars and authentic praise on Yelp and Angi. Yet the decision page shows five static quotes a stranger cannot verify. The proof exists; it is just not where buyers look. And 4.6 — the lowest in the set — is a number worth actively defending and lifting.

Gap 03 · No follow-up loop

Manual after the sale

You publish an SMS line, but I found no evidence of automated confirmations, reminders, or rebooking sequences. When follow-through depends on a person remembering, reviews about slow callbacks and unanswered issues follow — and each one drags a 4.6 lower.

Gap 04 · Invisible on paid search

Demand you never see

No Google Ads or Local Service Ads surfaced for RMP, and you do not rank for the high-intent terms incumbents own. Your reach is social-first — strong for brand, but it leaves the most valuable demand, people searching to hire right now, entirely uncaptured.

03The demand is already there

You are not short on demand. You are short on capture.

This is the part that makes RMP worth fixing rather than rebuilding. The hard thing — earning trust and creating demand — you already do. 103 Google reviews, a 4.0 reputation on Yelp, 4.4 on Angi, and roughly 667 Instagram followers say the work is good and people are paying attention. The reviews praise punctuality, honest diagnosis, and fixing what other plumbers could not. That is a quality base to amplify, not a quality problem to solve.

And the geography favors you. West Houston's Memorial, Briar Forest, and Energy Corridor corridor is full of the high-ticket work this trade lives on — whole-home repipes, tankless installs, water filtration. That is where I would aim paid capture, not the lower-ticket pockets nearby.

Royal-Pawz-style lift, in your dollars — illustrative

Assume RMP sees roughly 120 booking-intent actions a month (calls, site visits, Dispatch starts) and currently converts about 10% into booked jobs — that is ~12 jobs. At a blended $700 ticket, that is ~$8,400/mo. Lift the booking rate to ~20% — conservative, since Royal Pawz went from 7% to 30.8% — and you reach ~24 jobs, or ~$16,800/mo. That is roughly $8,400/mo of incremental revenue (~$100k/yr) from conversion alone, before adding a single dollar of new traffic.

Assumptions labeled and illustrative: intent volume and conversion rate are estimates to validate in the diagnostic; the $700 ticket sits inside the typical $300–$700 service-call to $1,500–$3,500 water-heater range for this vertical. Real inputs replace these in week one.
