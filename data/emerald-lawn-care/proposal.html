02 What it's costing you

Four places revenue leaks before it ever reaches Jobber.

None of these is a complaint about how you run jobs. They're all the same structural gap: there's no system turning demand into tracked, captured, fast-answered leads. Each one is fixable.

Gap 01 · No landing page

Nowhere to send a buyer

A landing page is the one asset everything else needs. Without it you can't run Google Ads, you can't rank in organic search, and you can't retarget. A "$X per cut, pick your date" page that feeds straight into Jobber would convert browsers who today bounce off a Facebook profile or a bare quote form.

Gap 02 · Invisible on paid search

Not in the auction at all

When a Cypress or Hockley homeowner searches "lawn care near me," the results are aggregator apps and established locals with sites. You're not in that auction. Lawn-care search clicks cost roughly $40–$120 per lead (industry benchmark) — affordable, but only once there's a page to send them to and a way to track the cost per booked client.

Gap 03 · No speed-to-lead

The first reply wins, and it isn't automated

In home services, the business that replies first usually books the job. Today a DM or missed call waits for you to see it. An automated text-back the moment a lead comes in — confirmations, reminders, "ready to book?" — is exactly what Royal Pawz used to lift conversions, and Jobber supports the hooks to run it.

Gap 04 · Thin, unharvested reviews

23 reviews under-sells a 4.8

Your rating is excellent; the volume is light for a recurring-service brand, and there's no automated review request after a completed job. More reviews lift both ad click-through and organic trust. This is a compounding asset you're already earning the right to and not collecting.
