02 What it's costing you

Four gaps between a click and a signed fence job.

None of these are about your craft or your reputation. Every one of them lives in the software layer between the lead and the calendar — the seam where revenue quietly drains.

Gap 01 · No instant response

The first to reply usually wins

A homeowner pricing a fence rarely asks one company. When your reply depends on a person reaching the callback queue, a competitor with an auto-text or instant quote answers first. In high-ticket trades, speed-to-lead is one of the strongest predictors of who closes the job.

Gap 02 · No after-hours capture

Evenings and weekends go cold

People shop for fencing after work and on Sundays. A form with no auto-confirmation and no scheduling means those leads land in an inbox until someone is back at a desk. By Monday, the warmest ones have moved on. A booking + confirmation flow captures them at the moment of intent.

Gap 03 · No SMS follow-up

The follow-up never gets sent

Most fence quotes do not close on first contact — they close on the second or third touch. With no automated SMS or email nurture, every follow-up depends on Cameron or a rep remembering to chase it by hand. Busy weeks mean warm leads simply go quiet. That is recoverable revenue, not lost demand.

Gap 04 · No conversion measurement

You can't fix what you can't see

I could not confirm conversion tracking (GA4, Meta Pixel, Google Ads tags) from the outside. Without it, you cannot see where leads drop in the four-step form, or which city pages convert. Today you are flying on instinct. The diagnostic puts numbers under it.

03 The demand is already there

You don't need more attention. You need to convert the attention you have.

100 five-star reviews is not a vanity number — it is proof of demand and a product worth pouring more leads into. Reviewers name Cameron directly, praise the stick-built precision, the clean job sites, the ~1.5-day turnarounds. That reputation already outranks Hill Fence on rating and out-reviews Custer Fence. None of your local competitors pair that trust with an instant-response booking layer. Whoever adds it first converts the same traffic at a far higher rate. That is the wedge.

And the geography is on your side. Memorial, Briar Forest, and the Energy Corridor are full of homeowners replacing aging fences on high-value lots — exactly the custom, high-ticket work you do best. A frictionless conversion layer earns you more of those jobs without buying a single extra click.

Illustrative ROI — your numbers, conservative assumptions

Assumptions (labeled, to be confirmed in the diagnostic): ~100 quote-form leads per month; current close rate ~10% = 10 jobs; conservative ~$5,000 average installed fence (West Houston residential wood/privacy installs typically run $3,500–$9,000+, custom and metal higher).

Today: 10 jobs × $5,000 = ~$50,000/month. Move the close rate from 10% to 18% on the same leads — through instant response plus automated follow-up — and you reach 18 jobs = ~$90,000/month. That is roughly +$40,000/month (~+$480,000/year) with zero extra ad spend.

An 18% close rate is deliberately conservative: Royal Pawz roughly quadrupled its booking rate with this exact pattern. Layer a managed Google Ads top-of-funnel on top and the upside compounds. For a high-ticket trade like fencing, even a few extra closed jobs a month dwarf any fee I would charge.
