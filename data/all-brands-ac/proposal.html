02 What it's costing you

Four gaps, each one a job you don't book.

None of these is a marketing problem in the usual sense. Your marketing already works — 23 hires on Thumbtack, repeat recommendations on Nextdoor, "Best Pick since 2024." These are software gaps sitting between your reputation and your calendar.

Gap 01 · Trust

Your social proof is invisible on your own site

You have 130-plus genuine five-star reviews spread across Thumbtack (4.9, 72 reviews), Best Pick (5.0, 62 ratings) and Angi. None of it is surfaced where it closes the sale — your own pages. A homeowner comparing you to The Chill Brothers sees their 1,000-plus Google reviews and your reviews page; the reputation you earned never gets to do its job.

Gap 02 · Capture

No way to book the job in the moment

"Book Online" sends a homeowner to a form, then to waiting. In peak Houston summer, a customer with a dead AC will book the first contractor with an open slot. With no calendar and no instant confirmation, every lead becomes a callback race you have to win by phone — and the ones you can't answer in minutes are gone.

Gap 03 · Follow-up

No SMS, no missed-call text-back

"Call or text 24/7" is you, by hand, around the clock. There is no automated text-back when a call is missed, no confirmation, no reminder, no rebooking nudge. A missed call at 9pm in July is a $900 job that goes to whoever texts back first. Right now nothing texts back for you.

Gap 04 · Measurement

No pixels, no analytics, no retargeting

There is no Google Analytics and no Meta Pixel on your site. You cannot see how many people reach the form and leave, you cannot retarget the homeowner who almost booked, and you can't run paid search profitably because you can't measure what a click is worth. You're flying with no instruments — and renting leads from Thumbtack instead of owning a funnel.
