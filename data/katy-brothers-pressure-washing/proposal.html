The single biggest leak

The customer who will not text, or who fills out the form at 9pm, hears nothing back until you are free — and your fastest-responding competitors are calling people back within minutes. 02What it is costing you

Four specific places revenue is leaking — none of them are about reputation.

Reputation is solved. What is missing is the system that turns a 5.0 rating into booked jobs without you personally answering every message. Here is where the money goes.

Leak 01 · Intake

No instant booking

The CTA on every page is "Request Quote." A motivated buyer cannot pick a slot, see a price range, or commit in the moment. They have to start a conversation and wait — friction that competitors with online estimates do not impose.

Leak 02 · Speed-to-lead

No after-hours capture

Form submissions and texts depend entirely on your availability. A lead that arrives during a job, in the evening, or on a weekend sits until you surface. In this market, reviewers report rivals calling back "within two minutes." Speed-to-lead is where the booking is won or lost.

Leak 03 · Follow-up

No automated nurture

There is no confirmation, reminder, or rebooking message. Every follow-up is you doing it by hand. Quotes that go cold are never automatically revived, and past customers are not nudged for the next seasonal clean — recurring revenue you have already earned the right to.

Leak 04 · Acquisition

Invisible on paid search

No Google Ads, no Meta ads, no retargeting pixel detected. You rank on your own name, but the high-intent non-branded searches — "pressure washing Katy," "soft wash Memorial" — go to competitors. And running two domains for one brand splits whatever SEO equity you do have.
