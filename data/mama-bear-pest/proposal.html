02 What it's costing you

Four gaps between a visitor and a booked job.

None of these are about effort or service quality — your reviews already prove those. They are structural gaps in the machine around the work.

Gap 01 · No front-door booking

New leads can't pick a time.

The "Book Now" page collects name, phone, address, and the problem — then ends. There is no slot to choose and no confirmation. A homeowner with a wasp nest today has no way to lock in tomorrow. The job that was ready to book becomes a callback queue, and a share of those leads cool off or call a competitor first.

Gap 02 · No speed-to-lead text

Silence after submit.

There is no automated text the moment a form comes in. In home services, the first business to respond usually wins the job. Without an instant "we've got your request, here's a time" message, you are competing on how fast a human happens to be free — not on the strength of your service.

Gap 03 · No paid acquisition

Nothing feeds the pipe.

I found no Google Ads and no Meta Pixel — so no paid search and no retargeting. Demand today is inbound: referrals, Nextdoor, and directory leads. That is a clean foundation, but it caps you at the volume word-of-mouth happens to produce. There is no dial you can turn up.

Gap 04 · Thin reviews, low reach

The story isn't being seen.

You have 70 five-star reviews and a real differentiation — honest, eco-friendly, family- and pet-safe, no annual lock-in contracts. But against Katy incumbents with 1,000+ reviews and page-one rankings, organic discovery is near zero. The problem isn't the story; it's how few people ever reach it.

Why this is good news

Every one of these is a system fix, not a rebuild. You already have the licensed operator, the product, the brand, and the scheduling software. The work is connecting them so a stranger can find you, pick a time, and get confirmed — without a human in the loop for the first reply.
