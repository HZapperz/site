- **Ticket size (their own pricing):** maid service from **$150**, deep clean from **$200**; recurring clients are the profit core (VERIFIED — site pricing). A West-Houston affluent home (Memorial/Briar Forest/Energy Corridor, larger sq ft) realistically runs **~$180–$300 per recurring visit**, and a biweekly recurring client is worth **~$4,000–$7,000+ in annual revenue** and multi-year LTV.
- **Paid-search economics (vertical benchmark, UNVERIFIED to this firm):** Houston house-cleaning Google Ads CPCs commonly run ~$6–$15; with a tuned instant-quote funnel, booked-customer CAC typically lands in the **~$80–$200** range. Against a multi-thousand-dollar recurring LTV, even conservative paid acquisition clears 3–5x+ ROAS — the same shape as the Royal Pawz outcome.
- **Seasonality:** Spring (deep-clean season), pre-holiday (Oct–Dec), and move season (summer) spike demand; Jan/late-summer soften. A paid layer lets them buy demand in the troughs.
- **Royal-Pawz-style math (illustrative, their dollars):** Their funnel is already built, so the lever is **volume**, not conversion rate. If Zapp drives an incremental **20 booked recurring clients/month** via paid + retargeting at ~$120 CAC, and each recurring client is worth ~$5,000/yr, that's **~$100,000 in new annual recurring revenue for ~$2,400/mo media** — a ~40x revenue-to-spend ratio before churn. Even discounting heavily, the ROI case is strong because the conversion infrastructure they'd normally pay Zapp to build **already exists** — Zapp's value here is the **traffic + retargeting + speed-to-lead layer on top**, not a ground-up build.
