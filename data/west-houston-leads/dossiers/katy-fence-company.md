## 7. Vertical economics & ROI model

- **Ticket size:** Houston-area wood/privacy fence runs **~$23–$45 per linear foot**; a typical residential job (~150 ft) lands **~$3,600–$6,300**, with cedar/iron and gates pushing higher. Call it a **conservative ~$4,500 average installed ticket** for residential.
- **Volume:** Site claims **600+ projects/year** (UNVERIFIED). Even at a conservative blend, that implies a **multi-million-dollar topline** — reinforcing that this is an established operator.
- **CAC / ROAS (West Houston fencing, paid search):** Fence keywords are competitive; expect **~$8–$20+ per click** and meaningful cost-per-qualified-lead, but with a $4,500 ticket and ~30–45% gross margin, even a few-hundred-dollar CAC yields strong ROAS. Seasonality: peak spring–early fall, **storm-driven surges** (post-hurricane fence replacement is a major demand spike in Houston).
- **Royal-Pawz-style conversion math:** Suppose they currently get **~120 form/call leads a month** and convert ~25% to booked jobs = **~30 jobs**. Lifting booked-conversion via instant SMS + online scheduling from 25% to even **40%** (a Royal-Pawz-scale lift) = **~48 jobs** — **+18 jobs/month**. At a $4,500 ticket and ~35% gross margin (~$1,575 gross/job), that's **~$28k incremental monthly profit / ~$340k/year** from the *existing* lead flow, before any new ad spend. The leverage here is **converting the demand they already earn**, not buying more traffic. (All figures illustrative — anchor to their real lead count in the Diagnostic.)
