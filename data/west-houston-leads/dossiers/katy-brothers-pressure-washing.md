## 7. Vertical economics & ROI model

- **Ticket size (VERIFIED-ish from sites/market):** Driveways advertised "starting at $89"; Katy market driveway jobs run ~$80-$200, full house soft-wash + roof + flatwork bundles commonly $300-$700+. Blended residential ticket ~**$250-$400**; HOA/commercial jobs much larger.
- **Paid-search CAC / ROAS (vertical benchmark, UNVERIFIED for this operator):** Pressure-washing Google Ads in suburban Houston typically run roughly $8-$20/click with ~5-12% landing-to-lead and decent close rates on warm exterior-cleaning intent; well-run campaigns target ~3-5x ROAS. Speed-to-lead and a tight landing page are the dominant levers -- exactly what's missing here.
- **Seasonality:** Strong spring-summer demand (pollen, mildew, pre-listing/curb-appeal); softer in winter. Texas heat extends the season vs. northern markets.
- **Royal-Pawz-style lift math (illustrative, UNVERIFIED inputs):** Say the two sites pull ~250 monthly visitors and currently convert ~8% to a quote request via the text/form path (~20 leads). Add instant online booking + auto speed-to-lead SMS and lift effective booking conversion toward ~20-25% (the Royal Pawz pattern of roughly doubling-plus). That's ~30-40 more booked jobs' worth of pipeline; at a ~$300 blended ticket, even **+15 incremental booked jobs/month = ~$4,500/mo (~$54K/yr)** of recovered revenue from traffic they already have -- before adding any paid traffic on top. Layering a $1-2K/mo Google Ads engine at 4x ROAS adds another ~$4-8K/mo. The conversion fix alone likely pays for the engagement.
