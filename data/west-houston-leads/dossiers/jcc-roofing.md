**Website:** myroofimprovement.com — **WordPress** (wp-content artifacts), purpose-built marketing site with dozens of SEO landing pages (per-service + per-city: /houston/, /roofing-houston/, /roof-replacement-in-houston/, /roof-repair-company-in-houston/, etc.). **Mobile / template artifacts:** Modern WordPress build; no obvious cheap-template tells (UENI/Wix/GoDaddy) found. Mobile experience to be confirmed live.

**Booking / lead capture:** This is where the seed's thesis collapses. They run a **comparatively sophisticated lead funnel**:
- A **4-step progressive contact form** (with a 25% completion progress bar) capturing property type, owner status, service type, address, stories, square-footage ranges, best-time-to-call, and project notes.
- A **separate streamlined "Schedule a Call" form** with discount-qualification fields (military / first responder / nurse / teacher / senior).
- An **integrated Roofr instant-quote calculator** ("Live & Interactive," 24/7 baseline estimates) — i.e., they already offer the very "frictionless online estimate" wedge Zapp sells.
- CTAs everywhere: "Schedule Your Free Inspection," "Book Your Service Now," "Get Your Instant Roof Quote," "Try Instant Quote," "Call (713) ROOFING."

**What's MISSING:** No real-time **calendar booking** (you can't self-book a slot — it's "request a callback"). No **live chat**. No **confirmed SMS/email auto-response/speed-to-lead automation** (a text line exists at (832) 590-3700 but appears manual). Payment: financing promoted ("0% APR up to 18 months, 100% financing") — appropriate for high-ticket roofing; no DIY online checkout (expected for this ticket size).

**SINGLE biggest conversion leak:** **Speed-to-lead / after-hours capture.** A multi-step form plus a "best time to call" field plus three different phone numbers implies **human, business-hours follow-up** (M-F 8-6, closed weekends). Roofing storm-damage leads are urgent and impulsive; the homeowner who fills the form Saturday night and gets no instant text/booking confirmation can be intercepted by an incumbent with instant booking. That is a **real but narrow optimization** — not a green-field "build them a funnel" opportunity.

## 7. Vertical Economics & ROI Model

- **Ticket size:** Roof **replacement** in Houston typically **$9,000–$30,000+** (large/complex Memorial & Energy Corridor homes skew to $20k–$45k+); repairs $400–$2,500.
- **Paid-search economics:** Roofing is one of the **most expensive PPC verticals** — Houston roofing keyword CPCs often **$15–$40+/click**, with cost-per-acquired-lead frequently **$80–$250** and booked-job CAC commonly **$500–$1,500+**. Even so, on a $15k–$25k replacement, a healthy ROAS is very achievable — which is exactly why JC&C already invests in SEO/instant-quote infrastructure.
- **Seasonality:** Storm/insurance-claim spikes (Houston hail/wind season spring–early summer; hurricane season Jun–Nov; the site name-checks Hurricane Beryl). Demand is event-driven and urgent → speed-to-lead matters more here than in steady-state services.
- **Royal-Pawz-style lift, applied to THEIR dollars:** A grooming-style "7%→30%" green-field jump does **not** translate — JC&C already converts well and isn't conversion-starved. The only honest math is a marginal speed-to-lead recovery: if they take, say, ~40 web leads/month and currently miss ~15% of after-hours leads to faster competitors, recovering even 3–4 of those into a single extra $18k replacement is ~$18k–$70k of recovered annual revenue. That is a **conversion-rate-optimization** value story, not a 50x-MRR transformation story — and JC&C is sophisticated enough to question whether they need an outside operator to add an SMS auto-responder.
