04The idea

Capture demand — don't rent traffic

Meta interrupts people who weren't shopping. Google catches people who already are. For a brand few people know yet, selling at a high price, the second is where the affordable, convertible demand lives.

Core · catch ready-to-buy demand

Google Search + Shopping

Someone typing "occasion dress," "resort wear dress," or a destination outfit has already decided to buy — they're just choosing where. Apparel converts ~4% on Google search vs ~3% on Meta in category averages, and Shopping clicks are cheap. This is the demand we can actually win.

Second · take over & grow

Optimize Meta, then expand

We inherit and fix the existing Meta/Instagram program from day one — better targeting, clean exclusions, retargeting the real visitors Google and organic bring in. Once it's working, we grow it as an active channel and test TikTok alongside. Google leads the spend; these build around it as results justify.

The caveat that keeps this honest

Google catches demand that already exists — it doesn't create it. A brand few people know yet has little branded demand to catch, so early results come from category terms (not cheap searches for our own name) — and those mass-market ~4% averages are a ceiling to aim at, not our starting rate for a $500 considered purchase. That's exactly why the brand-building half — organic content and a credible social presence, produced by Mary's team alongside (not inside) ad management — is the necessary partner to this plan. The closest comparable, Luca Faloni, moved >50% of budget to Google for ~30% lower customer-acquisition cost, but explicitly kept Instagram running for awareness. We propose the same split of roles, not abandoning social.

06Expectations

What to expect — and the numbers

The most important expectation to set with the board up front: for a brand like this, a sale is rarely a single click. Fund the plan and then judge it on week-two results, and you kill a good program early.

The rule of thumb is ~7 touches to convert a customer. For a new, unknown brand selling a $500 considered purchase, the real number runs higher — our planning assumption is 15+ meaningful touchpoints from first exposure to first order. That's consistent with the category: luxury fashion is a multi-session, multi-week decision. Three things follow:

  • The funnel has to be more than ads. Search + Shopping catches the moment someone's ready to buy; organic content (Mary's team), the email program, and a small retargeting touch supply the earlier exposures. Ads alone can't manufacture 15 touchpoints.
  • The first 8–12 weeks are a ramp, not a verdict. A brand-new account can't yet hit the order volume the bidding algorithms need, so it learns slowly. Early cost-per-order will be high and noisy — that's the price of building the data, not a sign the channel is wrong.
  • We judge on blended results, monthly — not on a daily dashboard (the how is in section 07). At single-digit monthly orders, day-to-day numbers are too noisy to steer by.

Against that backdrop, here's what ~$3–3.5k realistically does, month by month. Planning ranges, not a hockey stick — treat them as directional, because at this volume outcomes are genuinely uncertain.

Fully establishing a premium brand to stable profit is a much bigger, longer number — for context, roughly $100–300k over 12–18 months, assuming spend scales to ~$8–15k/mo plus content; that is not part of this ask.

07Accountability

How we measure it — and when we scale or stop

A measurement setup you can trust, that costs almost nothing — plus a clear rule in both directions: when spend goes up, and when we call it.

What we track

Total sales ÷ total spend, monthly

Blended MER — every dollar of revenue over every dollar of spend, a number no platform can inflate — cross-checked against a post-purchase "how did you hear about us?" survey. A short written read each month, not a daily-dashboard fire drill.

The free foundation

GA4 + Enhanced Conversions

First-party and free; recovers a meaningful share of conversions lost to privacy gaps (often cited up to ~10%, setup-dependent) and — more important at low volume — gives Google a cleaner signal to bid on. Customer Match from your email list means re-engagement still works even if Meta's pool is too thin.

The scale ruleup

Only after it's profitable

Only after ~2 straight months of a profitable blended return (~2×+) — i.e. once you're already profitable — and only by mutual written agreement, would we revisit budget. The fee doesn't change at this spend level; for a materially larger program (e.g. ~$6–8k+ spend) we'd discuss it then. Never automatically, never as a % of spend.

The stop ruledown

We'll say so in writing

If after the first couple of months tracking is clean but the program isn't on a credible path to breakeven, that's a signal to pause or re-scope — Mary & Adam make that call at any monthly review, and we'll recommend it in writing rather than ask for more time.

What Zapp is accountable for from day one

Clean tracking and correct US-only / team / bot exclusions; a built and approved program; disciplined query pruning; a monthly written read; and an honest stop recommendation when the data calls for one. The ramp is not a blank cheque — the ChatGPT pilot has a kill criterion, and so does the core program.

08The downside

The two real risks — said plainly

Every honest plan has a most-likely failure mode. Here are ours, sized, with what we do about each.

Risk 1 · demand ceiling

Capture can plateau

As covered in The idea, Google can only catch demand that already exists — and a little-known brand has only so much of it. Paid could plateau in the high single digits until organic and social build awareness alongside. What we do: keep the budget concentrated and efficient, and be explicit that growth past ~10–12 orders depends on the brand-building half, not more ad spend.

Risk 2 · creative dependency

A dependency outside the contract

The plan leans on Mary's social team delivering a steady creative/content cadence — which sits outside our scope. If it doesn't materialise, ad efficiency suffers. What we do: agree a minimum cadence up front, flag any shortfall in writing within two weeks of Review #1, and pause non-brand spend rather than burn it against weak creative.

ChatGPT & AI answers — what's free, what's a test

The takeaway on the free half
