Decision 01— of 03

Your Our Story page — a first-person draft.

Right now the page reads in third person ("At Pecan Moon, we believe…"). This draft puts it in your voice. ~280 words, reads in 90 seconds. Five small blanks for you to fill in — they're highlighted in gold below.

A note from Mary
A note from Mary —

I grew up between the village / the coast / __________1 and the kitchens and gardens of women who dressed with intention. Nothing rushed. Nothing disposable. A linen dress lasted twenty summers because someone took the time to make it well, and someone else took the time to wear it well.

When I moved to Austin, I kept looking for the way those women dressed and not finding it. There was beautiful, and there was casual, and there was expensive — but there wasn't considered. Not in a way I recognized.

So I started making the pieces I wanted to wear. One at a time, by hand, in an atelier I built two miles from my kitchen2. Each piece is named for a woman I love3: Angela, Maria, Seraphina, Elora. Their dresses, their stories, their summers in the Mediterranean / __________4.

I named the brand for the pecan trees that line the road home, and for the moon that watches over the women who came before me5. A small inheritance, given quietly.

If you find your way here, I'd love for you to come stay a while. Step inside the atelier. Read the dispatches when they come. Wear a piece for twenty summers, if it suits you.

— Mary
The five gold-highlighted spots — fill in or tell me what's true
  1. Where you grew up. The texture matters — village name, coastal town, region. ("Between the Adriatic and my grandmother's garden in Split" is a thousand times better than "in Europe.")
  2. Atelier location. "Two miles from my kitchen" is a guess. Could be "in a converted garage behind the house," "in a studio on East 5th," etc.
  3. Whether pieces are actually named after women you love. If not — what's the naming convention? Places, feelings, seasons?
  4. The geographic anchor of the customer/brand. Currently "the Mediterranean." If your inspiration sits somewhere else (Texas Hill Country? The Aegean? Marrakech?), tell me.
  5. Why "Pecan Moon." My guess: pecan = the trees on your road home; moon = something celestial / feminine / family. Replace with the true story.
Fill in the five blanks (a sentence each is plenty) and reply. Or if any line should be rewritten entirely, just say.
