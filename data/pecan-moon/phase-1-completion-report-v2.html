02 · Findings surfaced today

Two items re-scoped by what we saw on the ground.

KL-4 · Cart-abandon subject re-investigated

Customers are NOT receiving "Email #3 Subject"

The flow's first email is a live A/B test (started 19 days ago, win probability 50.01%):

· Variation A subject: "Still thinking it over?"

· Variation B subject: "You left something behind"

The "Email #3 Subject" string is the parent message's stored subject field, displayed on the flow canvas as a card preview line. It's a workspace UI artifact — not what customers see.

Editing the parent subject while the A/B test is Live is not exposed by Klaviyo's UI. Pausing the test would lose 19 days of data; waiting ~30 days for natural resolution will auto-update the canvas display to the winning variation's subject.

Recommendation: accept the placeholder display. Customer impact is zero.

SW-3 · Hero mobile fix — theme architecture

The proposed <source media> approach won't fit cleanly

theme/sections/video-hero.liquid (pulled 2026-05-28) uses JavaScript-driven <template> swapping: #mobile-video-template and #default-video-template, with a <div class="current-video-container"> populated by JS based on viewport. The section schema exposes a separate video_mobile upload, but no "hide on mobile" toggle.

Recommended fix: add two new schema settings — disable_mobile_video (boolean checkbox) + mobile_image (image_picker). In the .liquid, when the toggle is on, suppress the mobile <template> and render <img> in .current-video-container for viewport ≤ 750px. Push via shopify theme push --unpublished --theme="phase-1-mobile-hero-fix".

Blocker: Mary needs to (1) supply a hero poster image and (2) approve the schema-change approach vs. simply replacing the mobile video upload with a poster directly.
