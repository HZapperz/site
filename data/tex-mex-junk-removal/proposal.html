The single biggest leak

The manual text-a-photo quote loop. A visitor who is ready to book has to upload a photo, then wait for a human to come back with a price. That one wait does three things at once: it loses the speed-to-lead race to competitors with instant online quotes, it drops every after-hours and weekend lead until you have time to reply, and it leaves no automated nudge for the people who never hear back. You are paying — in attention you already bought — to fill a queue that leaks.

02 What it's costing you

Four gaps, all on top of real demand.

None of these are a marketing problem. They are software gaps — the seam between "they found you" and "they booked." Each one is quantifiable.

Gap 01 · No instant quote

Speed-to-lead, lost

Junk King and Clearout give a price the moment a customer asks. You give a promise of a reply "within 1 hour." In a same-day vertical, the first credible number usually wins the job. Every minute of wait is conversion you've already paid for, bleeding out.

Gap 02 · No after-hours capture

The 24-hour window leaks

Your form is open around the clock; your reply is not. A request that lands at 9pm Saturday sits until Marco can answer. An instant-quote plus auto-SMS confirms, prices, and holds that lead the second it arrives — even when no one is awake.

Gap 03 · No automated follow-up

Silent leads stay silent

There is no SMS sequence for the people who request a quote and go quiet. In junk removal that's a large share of every inbox. A two-touch automated follow-up is the cheapest revenue in the business — and right now it isn't running at all.

Gap 04 · No conversion tracking

You're flying without instruments

A GoDaddy form gives you no pixel, no conversion events, no view of where leads drop. You can't optimize what you can't see — and you can't safely buy paid search until the funnel underneath it is measured. Fix the instruments, then spend.
